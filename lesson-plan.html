Lesson Plans & Teaching Resources

Downloadable lesson plans designed for educators, researchers, and community facilitators. Each plan is anchored to a specific film or vignette, providing classroom guides, discussion questions, and activity worksheets rooted in Bhutan's highland heritage.

For educators: All lesson plans are free to download and use for non-commercial educational purposes. Select a lesson plan to view its linked resources and download the guide.

Vignette — In Search of the Missing Yaks

In Search of Kin: Visual Anthropology & Ethnography in the Highlands

Students explore human-animal kinship, ritual ecology, and the ethics of visual storytelling through the story of Chencho, Dawa, and thirty missing yaks in the Haa highlands.

Undergraduate / College 3 Hours Visual Anthropology

Film — Salt & Passes

Ancient Trade Routes of the Himalayas

Maps the historical salt-for-grain trade between Tibet and Bhutan and what its disappearance means for highland identity.

Grades 9–12 60 min History & Geography

Film — The Living Deity

Sacred Cham Dance & Buddhist Ritual

Examines the meaning of Tsechu festivals, the symbolism of mask dances, and their role in Bhutanese spiritual and community life.
