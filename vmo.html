Our Foundation

The principles that guide every decision we make as a heritage institution.

01 Vision

A World That Remembers

We envision a future where the living traditions, oral histories, and sacred ecological knowledge of Bhutan's highland communities are not lost to time — but are accessible, celebrated, and carried forward by every generation. The RTC Heritage Portal aspires to be the definitive digital home for highland Bhutanese heritage, bridging the remote valleys of Haa and Sephu with the global academic community.

02 Mission

Document. Preserve. Share.

Our mission is to ethically document the cultural and environmental wisdom of Bhutan's high-altitude communities through rigorous academic fieldwork. We create a sustainable, open-access digital repository — comprising films, photo essays, oral vignettes, and soundscapes — produced in close collaboration with the communities themselves, ensuring authenticity and reciprocity at every stage.

03 Objectives

Concrete Goals, Lasting Impact

From producing community-approved multimedia archives to training the next generation of Bhutanese digital ethnographers, our objectives are measurable and mission-driven. We work across three regions, maintain partnerships with Leiden University, and publish resources freely available to educators, researchers, and the public worldwide.

Our Objectives

Six actionable commitments that define how we work and what we stand for.

01

Ethical Community Documentation

Conduct year-round fieldwork in Haa, Sephu, and Thimphu with full informed consent, ensuring communities have ownership and final approval over all published materials.

Fieldwork
02

Open-Access Digital Repository

Maintain a freely accessible, searchable archive of films, photo essays, audio recordings, and vignettes — permanently hosted and regularly expanded by RTC faculty and students.

Archive
03

Academic Curriculum Integration

Develop structured lesson plans and teaching resources that allow secondary and tertiary educators across Bhutan to integrate highland heritage into their curricula.

Education
04

International Research Partnerships

Collaborate with Leiden University and other global institutions to elevate Bhutanese highland studies within international academic discourse and comparative heritage research.

Partnership
05

Student Researcher Training

Provide RTC students with hands-on training in ethnographic fieldwork, digital archiving, oral history methodology, and responsible cultural documentation practices.

Training
06

Multilingual Accessibility

Work toward making the portal fully accessible in both English and Dzongkha, so that Bhutanese communities can engage with and reclaim their own documented heritage.

Access

How We Work

Four core pillars that underpin every project, partnership, and publication we produce.

🌿

Authenticity First

Every film, essay, and recording is produced with and for the communities documented. We prioritize raw, unfiltered voices over polished narratives — because real heritage is never perfect, and that is precisely its power.

01
📚

Academic Rigor

All content is produced under the supervision of RTC faculty and reviewed against established ethnographic and digital humanities methodologies, ensuring the portal meets international scholarly standards.

02
🤝

Reciprocity & Consent

We return value to the communities we document — through shared copies of all materials, community screenings, co-authorship where appropriate, and a standing commitment to remove any content upon community request.

03
🌐

Global Accessibility

Heritage locked behind paywalls serves no one. All resources on the RTC Heritage Portal are freely available to educators, researchers, students, and the general public — anywhere in the world, at any time.
